Mantu Recipe

Home

Description

Afghan mantu is a traditional dumpling dish made by filling thinly rolled dough squares with a seasoned mixture of ground beef or lamb, finely chopped onions, garlic, salt, pepper, and a touch of coriander. The dumplings are folded and sealed, then steamed until tender. They're typically served layered with a savory tomato-based sauce made from sautéed onions, garlic, and crushed tomatoes, and topped with a cool, tangy garlic yogurt sauce. A final garnish of dried mint or fresh herbs adds a burst of flavor, making mantu a deliciously balanced dish of savory, creamy, and aromatic elements.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of flour
  • 3 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp vegetable oil
  • 2-3 cups of water
  • 1lb of ground beef or lamb
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tsp of black pepper
  • 1 1/2 cups of crushed tomatoes (or tomato sauce)
  • 1 cup plain yoghurt

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix 2 cups all-purpose flour with 1/2 tsp salt.
  2. Slowly add water (about 2/3 cup), kneading until a firm, smooth dough forms.
  3. Cover the dough with a cloth or plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  4. In a bowl, mix 1 lb ground beef or lamb, 1 finely chopped onion, 2 minced garlic cloves, 1 tsp salt, 1/2 tsp black pepper, and 1/2 tsp ground coriander.
  5. Divide the rested dough into smaller portions and roll each one thin (like pasta).
  6. Cut the dough into 3-inch squares.
  7. Place a small spoonful of the meat filling in the center of each square.
  8. Fold and pinch the edges together to seal tightly (like a small pouch or dumpling).
  9. Lightly oil a steamer basket or line it with parchment paper to prevent sticking.
  10. Place the mantu in the steamer, leaving space between each one and steam over boiling water for 30–40 minutes until fully cooked.
  11. In a pan, heat 1 tbsp oil and sauté 1 finely chopped onion until golden.
  12. Add 2 minced garlic cloves and stir for a minute.
  13. Pour in 1.5 cups crushed tomatoes, 1/2 tsp salt, 1/4 tsp turmeric, and black pepper.
  14. Simmer the sauce on low for 10–15 minutes until thickened.
  15. In a bowl, mix 1 cup plain yogurt with 1–2 minced garlic cloves and a pinch of salt.
  16. Place steamed mantu on a serving platter.
  17. Spoon tomato sauce over the dumplings, drizzle garlic yogurt on top.
  18. Garnish with dried mint, fresh cilantro, or red chili flakes if desired.
